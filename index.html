System at a glance

  • Source: Synapse / Databricks (legacy)
  • Bridge: OneLake Shortcuts (Zero-Copy Bronze)

Architecture

The key design principle was speed over purity: avoid heavy ingestion early, deliver trusted dashboards fast, then progressively modernize.

Decision #1 ‚Äî Zero-Copy Bronze

I avoided full ingestion to eliminate months of ETL work and reduce data movement costs, accepting temporary dependency on legacy systems.

Decision #2 ‚Äî Materialize Gold only

Gold was physically optimized in Fabric to guarantee Direct Lake performance while keeping Bronze/Silver lightweight.

Business challenge

The constraint

Reporting had to remain 24/7. A margin error of 0.05% could block financial close. A big-bang migration was too risky.

The risk

The initial lift & shift estimate was ~8 months and significant egress/duplication costs, with drift risk across two platforms.

Execution (roadmap)

  1. 1) Audit & governance

    Assessed the legacy platform, documented datasets, and created a data dictionary to align definitions across teams.

  2. 2) Hybrid foundation

    Configured Fabric Lakehouse + Shortcuts to legacy storage; established security and access patterns for coexistence.

  3. 3) Standardized build patterns

    Defined production standards for pipelines/notebooks (idempotence, watermarks, logging, repeatable deployments).

  4. 4) Gold materialization & performance

    Built incremental Gold datasets optimized for Direct Lake: partitioning, file sizing, and refresh strategy.

  5. 5) Invisible cutover

    Remapped Power BI semantic models to new views so business users saw no disruption‚Äîonly faster reports.

Reliability & risk management

Idempotence

Pipelines and notebooks were designed for safe re-runs (no duplicates) using batch IDs, merge logic, and watermarking.

Schema drift protection

Added upstream schema validation so unexpected legacy changes wouldn‚Äôt corrupt Fabric tables or break downstream models.

Reconciliation ‚Äúcircuit breaker‚Äù

Automated checks compare legacy vs Fabric. If revenue divergence exceeds a strict threshold, publication is blocked and alerts are triggered before reports reach finance stakeholders.
